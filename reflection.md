# Reflection on Profile Comparisons

- High-Energy Pop vs Chill Lofi: High-Energy Pop prioritized upbeat, happy songs with higher energy, while Chill Lofi shifted toward lower energy and more mellow tracks. This makes sense because the score gives large weight to both genre/mood match and closeness to the target energy.

- High-Energy Pop vs Deep Intense Rock: High-Energy Pop favored bright pop/happy songs, whereas Deep Intense Rock selected aggressive, intense rock tracks. The difference reflects how mood labels and high-energy targets can push recommendations into different emotional styles even when both profiles request energetic music.

- Chill Lofi vs Deep Intense Rock: Chill Lofi produced more relaxed, acoustic-leaning recommendations, while Deep Intense Rock emphasized hard-driving, intense songs. This comparison shows that the model is testing for both energy and mood together, and when the user asks for intensity plus rock, the output moves away from mellow lofi.
